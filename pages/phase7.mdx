## 7.2 The Technical Assessment (Sample Questions)

### Section A: Platform Logic
**Q1: A buyer from the Marketplace asks for a price on 2,000 MT of Wheat. What is your first action?**
* *A)* Send the current market price immediately.
* *B)* Trigger the **Intent Filter (Phase 01)** to verify their technical specs and port requirements.
* *C)* Call a transporter to check freight rates.
* **Correct Answer: B.** In bulk trade, we filter for specific intent before discussing price to avoid "Price-Checkers."

**Q2: You found a supplier for 500 MT of Turmeric through a cold call (outside OBAOL). How do you manage this in the system?**
* *A)* You can't; OBAOL only works for internal leads.
* *B)* Create an **External Order** in the Execution Panel to use OBAOL’s tracking and subflow features.
* **Correct Answer: B.** OBAOL is an Execution OS for both internal and external deals.

### Section B: Subflow Management
**Q3: When should you trigger the "Logistics Bidding War"?**
* *A)* As soon as the inquiry comes in.
* *B)* Only after Phase 04 (Physical Stock Audit) is completed.
* *C)* After the goods have been loaded.
* **Correct Answer: B.** We only bid for transport once we are 100% sure the stock exists and is verified.

**Q4: How do you verify the stock level of a warehouse you have never visited?**
* *A)* Trust the Warehouse Manager's phone call.
* *B)* Check the **Live Inventory Feature** on your panel, which is updated by the on-ground Associate.
* **Correct Answer: B.** Digital tracking replaces manual trust.

### Section C: The Inquiry Chain
**Q5: A supplier confirms they have 1,000 MT of Maize. What are the next two questions you ask to build an "Inquiry Chain"?**
* *Expected Answer:* 1. "Who is handling your **Packaging**, and can we move that to our subflow for better tracking?"
    2. "Do you need **Warehouse space (Oyo model)** for your next harvest cycle?"
